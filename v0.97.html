How is plastic made?

Plastics can be either synthetic or bio-based. Synthetic plastics are derived from fossil fuels like crude oil and natural gas, while bio-based plastics are made from renewable biological sources such as starches, sugars, and vegetable oils. Plastic manufacturing involves various processes, often requiring precise control of temperature and pressure.

One common method is casting, where a mold is first coated with a release agent and sometimes preheated. A thermosetting resin, typically mixed with a curing agent, is poured into the mold. The resin then undergoes curing—either chemically or with heat—until it solidifies. Once set, the mold is opened, and the part is removed. Any excess material, such as sprues, is trimmed or sanded off.

Although there are many methods of plastic processing—such as injection molding, extrusion, and blow molding—casting remains a versatile and cost-effective technique, especially for small production runs and complex shapes.

Current levels of plastic production

Humans produce more than 400,000,000 metric tons of plastic every year, which is about the weight of every single human on the planet. However, only 0.5% of all that plastic enters the ocean, making the weight of plastic that goes into our oceans 2,000,000 metric tons. The rising levels of plastic have been projected to grow over time, according to a recent study by an environmental research association.
